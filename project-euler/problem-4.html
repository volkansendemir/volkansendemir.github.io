A palindromic number reads the same both ways.

The largest palindrome made from the product of two 2-digit numbers is 9009 = 91 Ã— 99.

Find the largest palindrome made from the product of two 3-digit numbers.

Brute-force solution


def brute():
    max_mult = 0
    margin_top = 10
    margin_bot = 10
    while max_mult == 0:
        for i in list(range(lim_up - margin_top + 1, lim_up + 1))[::-1]:
            for j in list(range(lim_up - margin_bot + 1, lim_up + 1))[::-1]:
                mult = j * i
                mult_str = str(mult)
                if mult_str == mult_str[::-1]:
                    if max_mult < mult:
                        max_mult = mult
        if margin_top == margin_bot:
            margin_top = margin_top * 10
        else:
            margin_bot = margin_bot * 10
    print(max_mult)

Once again, this "brute" solution does approach strategically.
To check for symmetricity we need to turn our numbers into strings and check against their reverses.
As the question asks for the largest palindrome, we start looping from the largest number down.
The way the margin_top and margin_bot work is as follows:
These variables start from 10-10 each and increase to 100-10, 100-100, 1000-100, 1000-1000, 10000-1000, etc.
They are then deducted from lim_up so we are testing intervals 990-990, 900-990, 900-900, 90-900, etc.
As we are looking for the largest product, there is no sense in searching all the way down to 1.
This becomes more apparent when you try larger upper limits than the 999 in our case.
