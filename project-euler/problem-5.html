Smallest multiple

Solved on Sat, 17 Oct 2020
Uploaded on Tue, 15 Dec 2020

2520 is the smallest number that can be divided by each of the numbers from 1 to 10 without any remainder.

What is the smallest positive number that is evenly divisible by all of the numbers from 1 to 20?

Brute-force solution


def brute():
    roots = []
    root_scores = {}
    for i in list(range(2, lim_up + 1))[::-1]:
        isPrime = True
        for j in range(2, i):
            if not i == j:
                if i % j == 0: isPrime = False
        if isPrime:
            roots.append(i)
            root_scores[str(i)] = 1

    for i in range(2, lim_up + 1):
        num = i
        for root in roots:
            root_score = 0
            while num % root == 0:
                root_score = root_score + 1
                num = num / root
            if root_scores[str(root)] < root_score:
                root_scores[str(root)] = root_score

    result = 1
    for root in roots:
        for i in range(root_scores[str(root)]):
            result = result * root
    print(result)

In the first part of this solution, we find the integers smaller than our upper limit, that are prime.
This means numbers like 4 or 8 can be replaced by 2^2 or 2^3 respectively. Numbers like 12 can be replaced by 2^2 and 3^1.
Therefore we aim to find the integers that cannot be replaced by the multiplication of others, basically primes.
Primes smaller than 20 are 2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13, 17, and 19 get added both to a list and to a dictionary with 1 as their value.

In the second part, we use the primes we found in the first part to increment the values in the dictionary.
This time we do not discriminate whether the number is prime or not and aim to find the largest root score in our range.
For example, the largest root score of 2 in our range to 20 is 2^4 with 16. The largest score for 3 is 3^2 for 9 and 18.

Now that we have root scores for all the primes smaller than our upper limit, we can multiply them all to arrive at our result.
With this objective we multiply 2^4, 3^2, 5^1, 7^1, 11^1, 13^1, 17^1 and 19^1 to find our solution.

Again, thanks to the strategic formulation of this solution, increasing the upper limit is still tolerable.
Trying with 100 as the upper limit, for example, results in 69720375229712477164533808935312303556800.
Even trying the upper limit as 1,000 produces a result in less than a second.
